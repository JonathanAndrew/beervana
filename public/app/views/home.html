Fill 5 gallon kettle up with 3 gallons of water and heat it up to 180 degrees.

Hold kettle to 180 degress. Add your specialty grains for aroma and color into stepping bag and stepp grains for 20-30 minutes.

Pull out specialty grains once they have been steeping for 20-30 minutes. You have now created what is called "wort". Bring wort up to boiling point (220-235 degrees).

Now it is time to add your DME(Dry Malt Extract) or LME(Liquid Malt Extract, reduce thickness of LME by placing bottle in hot water). DME and LME are sugar/syrups that you add for the yeast to feed on. Pour either DME or LME into wart while CONTINUOUSLY STIRRING. Boil wort for 60 minutes(be sure to start a timer).

After 30 minutes of boiling it is now time to add hops (flavoring hops) pellet or leaf form. Add bittering hops at minute 55.

After 60 minutes of boiling reduce the temperature of the wort to 70-80 degrees by transfering wort into the fermentor(make sure to use a screen) and then cool either by immersion cooler or by cooling plate.

After cooled, top off fermenter to 5 gallons with DISTILLED water. Take sample of wart and find the original gravity by using hydrometer(write down number for later use).

Its time to pitch your yeast, sprinkle yeast on top of the wort whether you are using dry or liquid yeast(DO NOT STIR).

After pitching the yeast, put lid on fermenter, set air lock to allow air to go in. The primary fermentation has now begun which will take 10-14 days
